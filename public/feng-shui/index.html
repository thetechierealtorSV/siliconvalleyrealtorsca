Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Bagua in Feng Shui?

The Bagua is the eight-trigram map used in Compass-school Feng Shui: Li (South, fame), Kan (North, career), Zhen (East, family), Xun (Southeast, wealth), Kun (Southwest, relationships), Dui (West, creativity), Qian (Northwest, helpful people), and Gen (Northeast, knowledge).

What is Vastu Shastra?

Vastu Shastra is the traditional Indian science of architecture. Directions include Ishanya (NE, auspicious), Agneya (SE, fire/kitchen), Nairutya (SW, primary bedroom), and Vayavya (NW, movement).

What is Ishanya?

Ishanya is the northeast direction in Vastu, governed by Lord Shiva. A NE entrance or puja space is considered highly auspicious.

What is the Brahmasthan?

The Brahmasthan is the geometric center of the home in Vastu Shastra, it should stay open and free of heavy structures, bathrooms, or kitchens.

What is sha chi or a poison arrow?

Sha chi is harsh, cutting energy, usually caused by a straight road, corner, or roofline pointing directly at the home (including T-junction lots). Remedies: screening plants, curved paths, or a Bagua mirror at the entrance.

What does Facing Li (South) mean?

Facing Li means the front of the home points south (180°). Li governs fame, recognition, and clarity, and a south-facing home also receives the most direct sunlight.

What does Facing Kan (North) mean?

Facing Kan means the front points north (0°). Kan governs career and life path, associated with water and flow.

How is the Harmony Score calculated?

Bagua facing (0–30) + Vastu entrance (0–30) + interior flow, command position, kitchen, Brahmasthan (0–25) + sha chi avoidance (0–15) = 0–100.
